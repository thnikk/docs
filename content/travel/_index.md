There are 3 main ways to reduce key travel.

## Lower travel switches

This is the easiest option as it doesn't require any sort of modification, but provides the least flexibility. If you want lighter, heavier, tactile, or clicky switches, you're SOL unless someone starts making those switches.

## O-rings

This is another easy option. It has the benefit of quieting the bottoming out of keys, but a lot of people don't like the mushy feeling that it adds (I personally don't mind it.) Another limitation is that it doesn't work on switches like Kailh's choc and ALPS switches, since they have a completely different keycap mount.

## Ball bearings

I actually came up with this idea a little while ago, but it looks like someone else had the same idea in 2017. https://imgur.com/a/xmSVZ

The idea is to put ball bearings into the center hole of the switch so the stem bottoms out on the ball instead of the bottom of the hole.
